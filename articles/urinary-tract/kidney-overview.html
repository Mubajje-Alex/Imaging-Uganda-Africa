Renal Ultrasound: From Basics to Advanced Pathology

Why Renal Ultrasound is Essential in Africa

In resource-limited settings, renal ultrasound is the first-line tool for:

  • Detecting obstructive uropathy (hydronephrosis) in HIV/TB patients
  • Screening for schistosomiasis-induced bladder/kidney fibrosis
  • Evaluating acute kidney injury (prerenal vs. intrinsic causes)
AI-Powered Tip: "In Uganda, always rule out TB kidney if you see calcifications + parenchymal thinning."

African Renal Pathology Atlas

Schistosomiasis

Key Signs: Bladder wall thickening, ureteral strictures, "pipestem" calcifications.

Renal Tuberculosis

Key Signs: Moth-eaten calyces, dystrophic calcifications, parenchymal thinning.

HIV-Associated Nephropathy

Key Signs: Enlarged echogenic kidneys, loss of corticomedullary differentiation.
