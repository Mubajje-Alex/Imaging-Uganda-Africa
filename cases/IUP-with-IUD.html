Ultrasound Case: Intrauterine Pregnancy with IUD in situ

A 28-year-old female with known IUD in situ complaining of LAP and ammenorrhea for 7 weeks

Case Presentation

History

  • Chief Complaint: Lap and Amenorrhea.
  • History of Present Illness:
    • Copper IUD inserted 2 years prior
    • Last menstrual period 7 weeks ago
    • Lower abdominal pain
    • No vaginal bleeding
  • Past Medical History:
    • G2P1 (one previous vaginal delivery)
    • No previous ectopic pregnancy
    • No history of PID
  • Laboratory Findings:
    • β-hCG: 12,500 mIU/mL
    • Progesterone: 18 ng/mL

Physical Examination

  • Normal vital signs
  • Suprapubic tenderness
  • IUD strings not visible on speculum exam
NOTE: Check IUCD annually in scan to be safely protected from unwanted pregnancy.

Ultrasound findings

IUD in situ:

  • Gestational sac: 15mm mean diameter, normal yolk sac
  • Fetal pole: CRL 8mm with detectable cardiac activity
  • IUD location: Fundal position, adjacent to gestational sac
  • Adnexa: Normal ovaries, no free fluid

IUD in situ

  • An echogenic linear focus with posterior acaustic shadowing

Intrauterine Pregnancy with IUD

  • Normal intrauterine gestational sac (GS) containing a well-defined yolk sac
  • Hyperechoic linear IUD adjacent to sac

Viable Embryo with IUD

  • CRL 8mm with cardiac activity
  • IUD in close proximity to gestational sac
Diagnosis: Viable intrauterine pregnancy at 7 weeks gestation with copper IUD in situ.

Management Considerations

  • IUD removal: Recommended if strings visible (reduces risk of miscarriage from 50% to 25%)
  • Expectant management: If IUD cannot be safely removed, close monitoring required
  • Risks: Increased risk of spontaneous abortion (up to 50%), preterm delivery, chorioamnionitis
  • Follow-up: Serial ultrasounds to monitor pregnancy progression and IUD position

Differentials

  • Ectopic pregnancy: No intrauterine gestational sac, positive β-hCG
  • Retained IUD without pregnancy: Negative β-hCG, endometrial changes only
  • Failed IUD expulsion: Partial expulsion with pregnancy
  • Uterine perforation with IUD: IUD not within endometrial cavity
