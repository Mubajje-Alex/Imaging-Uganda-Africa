Ultrasound Case: Epididymal Head Cyst

A 35-year-old male with painless scrotal swelling

Case Presentation

History

  • Chief Complaint: Painless right scrotal swelling noticed 3 months ago
  • History of Present Illness:
    • Gradually increasing size of the swelling
    • No pain or tenderness
    • No history of trauma or infection
    • No urinary symptoms
  • Past Medical History: No significant medical history
  • Sexual History: Married, no fertility concerns

Physical Examination

  • Scrotal Exam: Smooth, round, fluctuant mass palpable at the head of the right epididymis
  • Transillumination: Positive (suggests fluid-filled lesion)
  • Testes: Normal size and consistency bilaterally
  • Vital Signs: Normal
Clinical Suspicion: Given the painless nature, positive transillumination, and location at the epididymal head, an epididymal cyst or spermatocele is suspected. Ultrasound is requested to confirm diagnosis and rule out other pathology.

Ultrasound findings

  • Well-defined anechoic cyst at the head of the epididymis
  • Thin walls with posterior acoustic enhancement
  • No internal vascularity on Doppler imaging
  • Normal testicular parenchyma with intact blood flow
  • Possible septations in larger cysts
  • Possible low-level echoes in spermatocele (if present)

1. Right epididymal head cyst

  • Anechoic cystic structure
  • Thin walls
  • Posterior acoustic enhancement

2. Right testis

  • Normal testicular parenchymal echopattern.
  • Normal testicular blood flow
Diagnosis: Epididymal Head Cyst (likely spermatocele)

Based on clinical findings (painless scrotal swelling, positive transillumination) and ultrasound features (anechoic cyst at epididymal head with no vascularity).

Differentials

  • Spermatocele: Essentially identical to epididymal cyst but contains sperm, may show low-level echoes.
  • Hydrocele: Fluid collection surrounding the testis, not limited to epididymal head.
  • Varicocele: Dilated pampiniform plexus veins, shows flow on Doppler.
  • Testicular cyst: Arises from testicular parenchyma, not epididymis.
  • Tunica albuginea cyst: Located within the layers of the tunica albuginea.
  • Epididymitis: Painful, shows hypervascularity on Doppler.

Management

  • Asymptomatic cysts: No treatment needed, reassurance only
  • Symptomatic cysts: Consider surgical excision (spermatocelectomy) if causing discomfort
  • Follow-up: Only if symptoms develop or significant size increase
  • Patient counseling: Explain benign nature and that it doesn't affect fertility unless very large
