Ultrasound Case: Pancreatic Pseudocyst

A 45-year-old male with history of recurrent pancreatitis presents with epigastric pain and early satiety

Case Presentation

History

  • Chief Complaint: Epigastric pain and early satiety for 2 weeks
  • History of Present Illness:
    • Dull, persistent epigastric pain radiating to back
    • Early satiety and 5kg weight loss over 2 months
    • No fever, nausea or vomiting
    • History of alcohol-related pancreatitis (3 episodes in past year)
  • Past Medical History:
    • Chronic pancreatitis
    • Type 2 diabetes mellitus
    • Hypertriglyceridemia
  • Laboratory Findings:
    • Amylase 120 U/L (normal 30-110)
    • Lipase 85 U/L (normal 7-60)
    • WBC 9.8 x10³/µL (normal)
    • CRP 32 mg/L (mildly elevated)

Physical Examination

  • Epigastric tenderness without guarding
  • Palpable epigastric mass
  • No jaundice or ascites

Ultrasound findings

Well-defined anechoic cystic lesion in pancreatic region:

  • Location: Anterior to pancreatic body
  • Size: 8.5 x 6.2 cm
  • Wall characteristics: Thin (1-2mm), smooth
  • Content: Anechoic with few internal septations
  • Doppler: No internal vascularity
  • Pancreatic parenchyma: Heterogeneous with calcifications

Pancreatic pseudocyst

  • Well-defined anechoic cystic lesion
  • Thin smooth walls

Chronic pancreatitis

  • Heterogeneous pancreatic echotexture
  • Parenchymal calcifications
Diagnosis: Pancreatic pseudocyst complicating chronic pancreatitis, confirmed by CT correlation.

Differentials

  • Mucinous cystic neoplasm: Thicker walls, internal septations, may have mural nodules.
  • Serous cystadenoma: Microcystic "honeycomb" appearance, central scar.
  • Pancreatic abscess: Thick irregular walls, internal debris, clinical signs of infection.
  • Lymphoepithelial cyst: Rare, usually asymptomatic, may contain keratin debris.
